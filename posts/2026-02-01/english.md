# This Week's Coffee Picks

This week, we're exploring the timeline of coffee arrivals. We've got a peak-season Ethiopian natural, bursting with fresh fruit, contrasted with a late-harvest Guatemalan washed coffee, offering deep, developed sweetness. See how timing affects the cup for both pour over and espresso.

---

## Pour Over Recommendations

### Pick 1: [ETHIOPIA | Yabitu Koba - Natural](https://nemesis.coffee/products/ethiopia-yabitu-koba-natural)

#### Coffee Profile
- Roaster: Nemesis
- Origin: Ethiopia Guji, Uraga Yabitu Koba
- Variety: Heirloom
- Process: Natural
- Roast: Light
- Roast Date: Recent
- Price: $27

#### Why Get This
① Seasonality/Status
- Peak season for Ethiopian naturals. This is when they're at their freshest and most vibrant.

② Rarity/Value
- A benchmark example of a Guji natural. Not rare, but an excellent value for a clean, classic profile.

#### Flavor Profile
**Variety Character**: Ethiopian heirlooms are known for their complex floral and fruit-forward cup profiles.

**Tastes Like**: Strawberry jam, lilac florals, fresh nectarine.

#### Brew Guide (V60/Pour Over)
- Ratio: 1:16
- Temp: 96°C (light roast needs high thermal energy for full extraction)
- Grind: Medium-fine

### Pick 2: [Antonio Tomás - Guatemala](https://matchstickyvr.com/products/akagera-copy)

#### Coffee Profile
- Roaster: Matchstick
- Origin: Guatemala Huehuetenango
- Variety: Caturra, Bourbon
- Process: Washed
- Roast: Medium
- Roast Date: Recent
- Price: $24.25

#### Why Get This
① Seasonality/Status
- Late-harvest arrival. This can mean more developed sugars and a rounder cup profile compared to early-crop lots.

② Rarity/Value
- A solid daily driver. Classic washed Guatemalan profile at a great price point.

#### Flavor Profile
**Variety Character**: Bourbon and Caturra varieties are prized for their balance, sweetness, and clean acidity.

**Tastes Like**: Ripe peach, soft caramel, sweet persimmon.

#### Brew Guide (V60/Pour Over)
- Ratio: 1:16
- Temp: 93°C (medium roast is more soluble, lower temp prevents over-extraction)
- Grind: Medium

## Espresso Recommendations

### Pick 1: [culture. | nemesis signature](https://nemesis.coffee/products/culture)

#### Coffee Profile
- Roaster: Nemesis
- Origin: Brazil, Ethiopia
- Variety: Blend
- Process: Natural
- Roast: Medium
- Roast Date: Recent
- Price: $20

#### Why Get This
① Seasonality/Status
- A seasonally-aware blend, combining fresh crop Ethiopian notes with the rich body from a recent Brazil harvest.

② Rarity/Value
- Great value for a versatile house blend. Designed for consistency and works well with or without milk.

#### Flavor Profile
**Variety Character**: A blend crafted for balance, heavy body, and classic chocolate/fruit notes.

**Tastes Like**: Toffee, mixed berry jam, nougat.

#### Brew Guide (Espresso Machine)
- Dose: 18g
- Ratio: 1:2 (for a ~36g shot)
- Temp: 93°C (standard for medium roast blends)
- Grind: Fine

### Pick 2: [Antonio Tomás - Guatemala](https://matchstickyvr.com/products/akagera-copy)

#### Coffee Profile
- Roaster: Matchstick
- Origin: Guatemala Huehuetenango
- Variety: Caturra, Bourbon
- Process: Washed
- Roast: Medium
- Roast Date: Recent
- Price: $24.25

#### Why Get This
① Seasonality/Status
- Late-harvest beans often make for sweet and forgiving single-origin espressos.

② Rarity/Value
- A great opportunity to try a balanced washed coffee as an espresso. Less challenging than a light roast SOE.

#### Flavor Profile
**Variety Character**: As espresso, the caramel and stone fruit notes become very concentrated and syrupy.

**Tastes Like**: Peach syrup, deep caramel, brown sugar.

#### Brew Guide (Espresso Machine)
- Dose: 18g
- Ratio: 1:2.2 (a slightly longer ratio helps open up the washed acidity)
- Temp: 92°C
- Grind: Fine

## Quick Guide
- **For a juicy, fruit-forward cup**: Nemesis Yabitu Koba (Pour Over)
- **For a balanced, daily brew**: Matchstick Guatemala (Pour Over/Espresso)
- **For a classic, rich latte**: Nemesis Culture Blend (Espresso)
- **For a sweet, clean SOE**: Matchstick Guatemala (Espresso)
