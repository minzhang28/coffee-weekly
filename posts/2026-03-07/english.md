# This Week's Coffee Picks

This week's theme is contrast. For pour over, we have a heavily processed, funky anaerobic Colombian against a classic, clean washed Guatemalan. For espresso, it's a sweet, nutty Brazilian single origin versus a juicy, complex seasonal blend from two origins.

---

## Pour Over Recommendations

### Pick 1: [Paraiso (Natural Anaerobic Caturra), Colombia](https://www.prototypecoffee.ca/shop/paraiso-natural-anaerobic-caturra-colombia)

![Paraiso (Natural Anaerobic Caturra), Colombia](https://images.squarespace-cdn.com/content/v1/5ae73c0125bf0240808fc40e/95617505-43f2-4ef8-a066-b8b0d4bca8e7/IMG_8820.JPG)


#### Coffee Profile
- Roaster: Prototype
- Origin: Colombia
- Variety: Caturra
- Process: Natural Anaerobic
- Roast: Light/Medium
- Roast Date: Recent
- Price: $28 / 250g

#### Why Get This
① [Seasonality/Status]
- Peak season, showing vibrant fruit expression.

② [Rarity/Value]
- A good example of a modern, process-forward Colombian. Expect heavy fermentation notes.

#### Flavor Profile
**Variety Character**: Caturra provides a good sweetness base, but the processing dominates the profile.

**Tastes Like**: Watermelon candy, blackberry jam, overripe mango. Can get funky if brewed wrong.

#### Brew Guide (V60/Pour Over)
- Ratio: 1:16
- Temp: 93°C (Anaerobics can get funky if pushed with too much heat)
- Grind: Medium-coarse

### Pick 2: [GUATEMALA | Familia Zelaya - Villa Sarchi](https://nemesis.coffee/products/guatemala-familia-zelaya-villa-sarchi)

![GUATEMALA | Familia Zelaya - Villa Sarchi](https://cdn.shopify.com/s/files/1/0570/9499/6149/files/Green_Front.png?v=1771528692)


#### Coffee Profile
- Roaster: Nemesis
- Origin: Guatemala Antigua
- Variety: Villa Sarchi
- Process: Washed
- Roast: Light/Medium
- Roast Date: Recent
- Price: $24 / 250g

#### Why Get This
① [Seasonality/Status]
- Peak season arrival from Antigua, super fresh.

② [Rarity/Value]
- Excellent value for a clean, classic washed coffee. A very reliable daily drinker.

#### Flavor Profile
**Variety Character**: Villa Sarchi is known for its delicate acidity and clean sweetness.

**Tastes Like**: Blackberry, dried apple, praline, with a milk chocolate finish.

#### Brew Guide (V60/Pour Over)
- Ratio: 1:16
- Temp: 96°C (High-density bean. Needs high temp to extract sweetness properly.)
- Grind: Medium

## Espresso Recommendations

### Pick 1: [DAK Moucha Mousse *Natural Espresso*](https://revolvercoffee.ca/products/dak-moucha-mousse-bulk-1kg-natural-espresso)

![DAK Moucha Mousse BULK 1KG *Natural Espresso*](https://cdn.shopify.com/s/files/1/0023/8593/6496/files/Screenshot2025-12-08at12.31.16PM_c249cae5-1c7c-4668-80ad-c9744e1dd99a.png?v=1765230796)


#### Coffee Profile
- Roaster: DAK (sold via Revolver)
- Origin: Brazil
- Variety: Acaia
- Process: Natural
- Roast: Medium
- Roast Date: Recent
- Price: $95 / 1kg

#### Why Get This
① [Seasonality/Status]
- Fresh harvest arrival from Brazil.

② [Rarity/Value]
- Microlot. Built for milk drinks and very forgiving to dial in.

#### Flavor Profile
**Variety Character**: The Acaia variety is known for high productivity and sweetness.

**Tastes Like**: Mocha, hazelnut spread, caramel. A classic profile for milk-based drinks.

#### Brew Guide (Espresso Machine)
- Dose: 18g
- Ratio: 1:2.2
- Temp: 93°C (Keeps the natural process sweetness in check, avoids over-extracting fermented notes)
- Grind: Fine

![Top Shelf - Seasonal Blend: Honduras + Brazil](https://cdn.shopify.com/s/files/1/0424/9482/0510/files/300gTopShelf_66319b54-5ecf-475c-9ea5-1ecd24bb15ec.png?v=1771371654)

- Grind: Fine

## How to Choose

For a wild, fruit-forward pour over, grab the Prototype Paraiso. If you prefer a clean, balanced cup, the Nemesis Guatemala is a perfect daily driver. For espresso, the DAK Moucha Mousse is a dream in flat whites, all chocolate and nuts. The Pallet Top Shelf blend is for those who want a juicier, more complex shot that can still stand up to milk.
