# This Week's Coffee Picks

This week's theme is "Peak Season Picks." Coffees from Brazil and Costa Rica are at their best right now—freshly arrived and full of flavour. We've selected two pour over and two espresso options that represent the best of what these origins offer.

---

## Pour Over Recommendations

### Pick 1: [DAK Moucha Mousse](https://revolvercoffee.ca/products/dak-moucha-mousse-bulk-1kg-natural-espresso)

#### Coffee Profile
- Roaster: Revolver
- Origin: Brazil (Natalia Moreira Brito)
- Variety: Acaia
- Process: Natural
- Roast: Medium
- Roast Date: Feb 4, 2026
- Price: $95 / 1kg

#### Why Get This
① [Seasonality/Status]
- Fresh Arrival. Brazil's peak harvest and shipping window is Jan-Apr, so these beans are in prime condition.

② [Rarity/Value]
- Great for home. The 1kg bag offers solid value for a daily drinker.

#### Flavor Profile
**Variety Character**: Acaia is known for high sweetness and a heavy body, typical of Brazilian coffee.

**Tastes Like**: Mocha, vanilla bean, raw almond, caramel sauce, hazelnuts.

#### Brew Guide (V60/Pour Over)
- Ratio: 1:16
- Temp: 94°C (lower temp controls the funk on this natural)
- Grind: Medium-coarse (like coarse sea salt)

## How to Choose

If you want a complex, dessert-like pour over, the DAK Moucha Mousse is a great pick. For a cup with more fruit and a clean honey sweetness, the Ivan Solis from Costa Rica is at its seasonal peak. For espresso, the Ex Animo Hooked is your bulletproof daily driver for milk drinks—classic chocolate and caramel. If you're pulling single origin shots, the Ivan Solis offers a brighter, fruit-forward experience.
